# Related Work and Literature Review
## Perceptual & Cognitive Mapping System

### Introduction

This review synthesizes existing research relevant to the dimensional approach to cognitive assessment, covering theoretical foundations, methodological precedents, and technological innovations that inform the development of the Perceptual & Cognitive Mapping System (PCMS).

### Theoretical Foundations

#### Dimensional Models of Personality and Cognition

**Five-Factor Model (FFM)**
- Costa & McCrae (1992) established the five-factor model of personality
- Demonstrated that personality traits exist on continuous spectra
- Provided precedent for dimensional over categorical approaches
- Limitations: Focus on personality rather than cognition

**Big Five Inventory (BFI)**
- John & Srivastava (1999) developed efficient dimensional assessment
- 44-item instrument measuring five continuous dimensions
- Demonstrated reliability and validity across cultures
- Methodological influence on PCMS item development

**HEXACO Model**
- Ashton & Lee (2007) proposed six-factor personality model
- Added Honesty-Humility dimension to FFM
- Demonstrated cross-cultural validity
- Structural inspiration for **multi-dimensional** (PCMS uses a **ten-dimensional routing** space, F–V; see codebase `cognitive-dimensions.ts`) cognitive assessment

#### Cognitive Assessment Evolution

**Traditional Intelligence Testing**
- Binet-Simon scale (1905) established intelligence measurement
- Wechsler scales (1939) introduced multiple cognitive indices
- Limitations: IQ scores as categorical labels
- Cultural bias concerns in traditional testing

**Neuropsychological Assessment**
- Luria (1966) introduced functional systems approach
- Lezak (1995) emphasized process over outcome
- Move toward dimensional cognitive profiling
- Foundation for multi-dimensional cognitive assessment

**Executive Function Assessment**
- Miyake et al. (2000) identified three executive function components
- Demonstrated related but distinct cognitive processes
- Provided precedent for cognitive factor analysis
- Methodological influence on dimension identification

#### Information Theory in Assessment

**Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT)**
- Lord (1980) pioneered item response theory applications
- Weiss (1982) developed adaptive testing algorithms
- Maximizes information gain per item
- Direct methodological predecessor to PCMS adaptive engine

**Information-Theoretic Question Selection**
- Chang & Ying (1996) introduced Kullback-Leibler divergence for CAT
- Van der Linden (1998) optimized information-based selection
- Foundation for PCMS information gain calculations
- Mathematical framework for adaptive assessment

### Methodological Precedents

#### Adaptive Questionnaire Systems

**Computerized Adaptive Personality Assessment**
- Reise & Waller (2003) applied CAT to personality assessment
- Demonstrated efficiency gains over fixed-length tests
- Established validity of adaptive personality testing
- Direct precedent for PCMS adaptive questionnaire

**Adaptive Clinical Assessment**
- Fliege et al. (2005) developed adaptive depression screening
- Reduced assessment burden while maintaining validity
- Demonstrated feasibility in mental health contexts
- Ethical considerations for adaptive systems

**Smart Questionnaire Systems**
- Gibson (2015) implemented dynamic question selection
- Used machine learning for question optimization
- Real-time adaptation based on user responses
- Technical foundation for PCMS adaptive algorithms

#### Dimensional Assessment in Clinical Contexts

**Research Domain Criteria (RDoC)**
- Insel et al. (2010) proposed dimensional approach to mental health
- Replaced categorical diagnoses with dimensional measures
- National Institute of Mental Health endorsement
- Theoretical validation of dimensional approach

**Hierarchical Taxonomy of Psychopathology (HiTOP)**
- Kotov et al. (2017) developed dimensional psychopathology model
- Empirically derived factor structure
- Demonstrated superior predictive validity
- Scientific precedent for dimensional classification

**DSM-5 Alternative Model for Personality Disorders**
- American Psychiatric Association (2013) included dimensional model
- Level of personality functioning scale
- Five trait domains assessment
- Clinical acceptance of dimensional approaches

### Gaps and Limitations in Existing Work

#### Known Headwinds for Dimensional / Non-Diagnostic Approaches

Projects taking similar approaches have encountered specific criticisms. PCMS addresses each:

**"Dimensional profiling is used to avoid giving diagnoses"**
Several neurodiversity profiling tools have been criticised for acting as diagnostic gatekeepers (see Portsmouth ND Profiling Tool controversy, 2022–2024). PCMS avoids this by: (a) explicitly encouraging users to seek clinical assessment if warranted; (b) never framing results in terms of diagnostic likelihood; (c) having no institutional pathway — results are personal.

**"Commercial interests drive the dimensional framing"**
PCMS is MIT-licensed with no commercial model. Research data is available to the academic community under data-sharing agreements.

**"The dimensions are arbitrary"**
Each of the ten routing dimensions F–V is grounded in peer-reviewed psychological constructs with published measurement instruments (cited in whitepaper §3). Weights are currently pilot estimates pending IRT calibration, and this is transparently documented.

**"Myers-Briggs dressed up as science"**
Key distinctions from commercial personality tools: (a) PCMS uses Bayesian confidence tracking, not fixed item sets; (b) no archetypes, no personality types — only continuous profiles; (c) open data, open code, open science; (d) explicit uncertainty quantification; (e) cultural adaptation with empirical DIF testing planned.

**"Cross-cultural tools impose Western constructs"**
Addressed by: (a) separate question banks developed with local consultants; (b) Twi localisation (draft); (c) SMS/offline path for low-bandwidth Ghana contexts; (d) planned DIF analysis; (e) cross-cultural measurement invariance testing in validation roadmap.
